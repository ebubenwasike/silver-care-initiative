🚨 Emergency Information

When to Go to Emergency

  • Chest pain or difficulty breathing
  • Severe bleeding or head injury
  • Sudden weakness or numbness
  • Severe burns or broken bones
  • Overdose or poisoning

Urgent Care Centers

For non-life-threatening conditions like:

  • Sprains and strains
  • Minor cuts requiring stitches
  • Ear infections
  • Urinary tract infections
  • Minor fractures
Remember: In case of life-threatening emergencies, call 911 immediately or go to the nearest emergency department.
